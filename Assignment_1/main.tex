\section{Problem}
Suppose that 5$\%$ of men and 0.25$\%$ of women have grey hair. A grey haired person is selected at random. What is the probability of this person being     male? Assume that there are equal number of males and females.
\section{Solution}
Let A={0,1} represent the random variable for being male or female and G={0,1} represent having grey hair or not.Then,\\
  \begin{align}
      P(A=0)&=50\%=0.5\\
      P(A=1)&=50\%==0.5\\
      P(G=1|A=0)&=5\%=0.05\\
      P(G=1|A=1)&=0.25\%=0.0025
  \end{align}
  By Bayes rules,
  \begin{align}
      P(A=0|G=1)&=\frac{P(A=0)\times P(G=1|A=0)}{P(A=0)\times P(G=1|A=0)+P(A=1)\times P(G=1|A=1)}\\
      P(A=0|G=1)&=\frac{0.5\times 0.05}{0.5 \times 0.05 +0.5 \times 0.0025}\\
      P(A=0|G=1)&=0.952380952
  \end{align}
  Probability that the grey haired person selected at random is male is approximately 0.95.
